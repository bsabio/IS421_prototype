Acme AI Secures $15M Series A Backed by Venture Partners

OrdoAI News Desk • 2026-02-05

Brooklyn-based fintech startup PayFlow has raised $8 million in a seed funding round led by NYC Ventures, marking a significant milestone for the company as it aims to develop next-generation payment infrastructure tailored for small businesses. The announcement was made on February 4, 2026, highlighting growing investor interest in financial technology solutions that support local commerce.

NYC Ventures, a venture capital firm with a focus on New York City-based startups, took the lead in this round, signaling confidence in PayFlow’s vision and potential impact on the small business sector. Fintech Capital also participated in the funding, joining forces to back PayFlow’s mission to modernize payment systems and streamline financial operations for smaller enterprises.

PayFlow’s approach centers on creating innovative payment solutions that address the unique challenges faced by small businesses, which often struggle with outdated or inefficient financial tools. By building infrastructure that is both accessible and scalable, the company hopes to empower merchants to manage transactions more effectively and compete in an increasingly digital economy.

The $8 million seed round will provide PayFlow with the resources needed to accelerate product development, expand its team, and deepen its market presence. Early-stage funding of this size reflects a strong vote of confidence from investors who see the potential for fintech innovations to drive economic growth within local communities.

This investment also underscores a broader trend of venture capital firms focusing on startups that serve small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), recognizing their critical role in the economy and the gap in tailored financial services available to them. By supporting companies like PayFlow, investors are betting on technology to transform how small businesses handle payments and financial management.

As PayFlow moves forward with its plans, the company is positioned to become a key player in the fintech ecosystem, particularly within New York City’s vibrant startup scene. The backing from NYC Ventures and Fintech Capital not only provides capital but also access to networks and expertise that can help the company scale effectively.

With this new funding, PayFlow is set to make strides in delivering next-generation payment infrastructure that could redefine how small businesses operate financially, potentially setting a new standard for the industry.
