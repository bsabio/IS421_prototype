CyberShield Secures $12M Series A Backed by Security Ventures

OrdoAI News Desk • 2026-02-01

MediCare, a growing telemedicine platform, announced on February 3, 2026, that it has secured $20 million in a Series B funding round. The round was led by Health Ventures, a prominent healthcare-focused investment firm, with additional participation from Impact Capital and Strategic Investors. This infusion of capital is expected to accelerate MediCare’s mission to expand access to telemedicine services, particularly targeting underserved communities across the United States.

The $20 million Series B round marks a significant milestone for MediCare as it looks to build on its initial success in the telehealth sector. By partnering with investors who specialize in healthcare, the company aims to leverage both financial resources and industry expertise to enhance its platform. Health Ventures’ leadership in the round underscores the growing confidence in MediCare’s approach to addressing gaps in healthcare accessibility.

Telemedicine has become an increasingly vital component of healthcare delivery, especially in areas where medical services are limited or difficult to reach. MediCare’s platform focuses on bridging these gaps by providing virtual consultations and remote care options, which can improve patient outcomes and reduce the strain on traditional healthcare facilities. The fresh capital will enable the company to scale its technology and expand its reach to more communities in need.

Impact Capital and Strategic Investors, who also contributed to the funding round, bring additional support and strategic guidance to MediCare’s growth plans. Their involvement signals a broader interest in telehealth solutions that can adapt to the evolving demands of patients and providers alike. As the healthcare landscape continues to shift toward digital solutions, companies like MediCare are positioned to play a crucial role in shaping the future of care delivery.

With this new funding, MediCare is poised to accelerate its expansion efforts and further develop its telemedicine offerings. The company’s focus on underserved populations highlights a commitment to equity in healthcare, aiming to make quality medical services more accessible regardless of geographic or socioeconomic barriers.

Overall, the $20 million Series B round led by Health Ventures reflects strong investor confidence in MediCare’s vision and potential. As telemedicine becomes an integral part of healthcare systems nationwide, MediCare’s growth trajectory could have a lasting impact on how patients receive care in the years ahead.
