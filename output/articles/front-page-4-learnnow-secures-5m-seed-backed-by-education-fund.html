LearnNow Secures $5M Seed Backed by Education Fund

AI Factory News Desk • 2026-02-02

LearnNow, a New York-based startup focused on revolutionizing education, announced on February 2, 2026, that it has secured $5 million in a seed funding round. The round was led by Education Fund, a prominent investor specializing in education technology, with additional participation from Angel Network, a well-known group of early-stage investors.

The company aims to transform the K-12 learning experience by developing an AI-powered personalized learning platform. This technology is designed to adapt to each student’s unique needs, helping to improve engagement and academic outcomes. By leveraging artificial intelligence, LearnNow hopes to provide tailored educational content that supports diverse learning styles and paces.

The $5 million seed round will enable LearnNow to accelerate product development and expand its team as it prepares to bring its platform to schools and families nationwide. Education Fund’s leadership in the round underscores the growing interest in technology-driven solutions that address longstanding challenges in education.

LearnNow’s approach reflects a broader trend in the education sector, where personalized learning powered by AI is gaining traction as a way to enhance student achievement and close learning gaps. With this fresh capital, the startup is well-positioned to build out its platform’s capabilities and scale its impact.

Angel Network’s involvement also signals confidence from experienced early-stage investors who see potential in LearnNow’s vision. The combination of specialized education funding and angel investment provides a strong foundation for the company’s next phase of growth.

As schools continue to seek innovative tools to support teachers and students, LearnNow’s AI-driven platform could play a significant role in shaping the future of K-12 education. The company’s successful seed round marks an important milestone on its journey to transform how students learn and succeed in the classroom.
